# Getting Married and Going on My Honeymoon

*May 16, 2026*

---

Hey everyone! I'm getting ready to get married to the love of my life. It is also my best man's birthday (unfortunate scheduling on my part lol). Around a year ago, I graduated from Radford University, and shortly after walking across the stage I asked my girlfriend to marry me (*May 10, 2025*). We spent around a year planning (mostly her), and man it was A LOT. She did an amazing job though, and the wedding rehearsal tonight went pretty well. We had some delicious food, practiced the ceremony, and even played the game where you try to guess which hand is your partner's while blindfolded. Overall, we had a great time!

## The Wedding
We are getting married at a place called *The Barn at Edwards Farm*. It is quite literally exactly what it sounds like. It is a barn, but quite upscale, with lots of space. We will be spending the day with around 130 of our friends and family, and although I'm nervous...I'm definitely very excited! We have some of our best friends and family in the wedding party, and I can't wait to marry the woman of my dreams!

## Honeymoon
As for our honeymoon, we are going on a Carnival cruise together. We have been on two cruises together in the past, but this one will be our first one entirely on our own and also the first one where we are staying in an ocean suite instead of a balcony room. I can't wait for it to get started, but we have a ~9 hour drive to complete first.

## What I'm Taking Away
I'm looking forward to tackling life with the woman I love. I'm very excited to finally get to live together and see each other more than a couple of times a week! However, I am a bit curious to see what all will change. For example, living on my own (or with my old roomate) I typically would come home from work and simply eat, play video games, and talk to my friends. I'm sure I will miss that, but I genuinely can't wait to spend more time with my soon-to-be-wife. I also look forward to getting on a more regular schedule. Living a little under an hour apart required quite a bit of planning for us to see each other, and that also resulted in some randomization in when we would get to see each other. However, I think that once the wedding is over we will be able to/need to develop a more fixed schedule. Some of the things I look forward to the most:
* Watching TV together
* Setting up the house together
* Going to church together
